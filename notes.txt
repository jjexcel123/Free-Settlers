Gold Rush Mining Techniques
Diggers searched for gold using pans or sluice boxes.
Chinese miners also extracted other metals like tin, copper, and tungsten
Immigration Waves and Statistics
Chinese immigration increased significantly when gold was first discovered in Australia.
Most migrants had to go into debt to travel to Australia.
The highest number of arrivals in a single year was 12,396 in 1856.
By 1861, there were over 24,000 Chinese immigrants on the Victorian goldfields.
In 1861, China-born residents totaled 38,258 people, making up 3.3% of the Australian population.
A new influx of Chinese miners arrived in the 1870s following gold discoveries in Cooktown.
Social Impact and Legislation
Chinese migration was heavily shaped by restrictive laws and changing social attitudes.
The large presence of Chinese miners caused tensions with European miners.
Post-Gold Rush Occupations
Many Chinese immigrants returned to China after the gold rush ended.
Those who stayed in Australia took up new occupations, such as market gardening.
